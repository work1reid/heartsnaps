Photo Magnets as Gifts for Grandparents: Ideas for Every Occasion

March 2026 7 min read Gift Ideas
👵

There's a specific kind of joy that happens when you give a grandparent a photo of their grandkids. Their face lights up. They hold it close. They immediately start figuring out where to put it.

Photo magnets go straight on the fridge. And that's exactly where grandparents want their family photos — somewhere they see them every single day, multiple times a day, without even trying.

Why Grandparents Love Photo Magnets

We hear it constantly from customers: "Mum absolutely loved them" and "Pop has them right in the middle of the fridge."

Here's why photo magnets are the perfect grandparent gift:

  • They're visible every day. A photo frame sits on a shelf. A photo album goes in a drawer. A magnet lives on the fridge — the most-visited spot in any home.
  • No tech required. No apps, no passwords, no charging. Just a photo they can see and touch.
  • They're not "stuff." Grandparents often say "don't buy me anything." Photo magnets aren't stuff — they're memories.
  • They can be updated. Order a new set each year — new school photos, new milestones, new memories. The fridge becomes a timeline.
  • They're conversation starters. When friends visit, the fridge photos become the first talking point: "That's our grandson, he just started school."

The Best Occasions to Gift Photo Magnets

Mother's Day & Father's Day

The most popular time we make magnets for grandparents. A 6-pack of the grandkids' latest photos is the gift Nan actually wants — not another candle or box of chocolates.

Christmas

Update the fridge collection with the year's best moments. Family Christmas photo, school photos, holiday snaps. Some families make it a tradition: new magnets for the grandparents every Christmas.

Birthdays

A set of magnets for a milestone birthday — 60th, 70th, 80th — is deeply personal and always appreciated. Include a mix of new photos and throwback favourites.

New Baby Announcement

Just had a baby? The hospital photos, the first close-up of that tiny face, the newborn held by a sibling — these become treasured fridge magnets. Grandparents will show every visitor.

Grandparents Day (October)

A smaller occasion, but that's what makes it special. A magnet of the grandkids with a quick note is a thoughtful surprise they won't expect.

Moving to Aged Care or a Retirement Village

This one is close to our hearts. When a grandparent moves to a smaller space — a retirement unit or aged care facility — wall space is often limited. But there's always a fridge. Photo magnets become the primary way they display family photos, and it means the world to them to see familiar faces every day in an unfamiliar place.

Best Photos to Choose

Close-ups of grandkids

This is the number one winner. A clear, close-up photo of each grandchild's face — smiling, laughing, being themselves. One magnet per grandchild is a beautiful set.

Grandparent + grandchild together

If you have a lovely photo of Pop holding the baby, or Nan reading to the kids, or all of them together — that's pure gold for a magnet.

Milestone moments

First day of school. Lost tooth. Birthday party. Dance recital. Sports day. These are the moments grandparents want to keep close.

Pet photos

Don't underestimate this one. If Nan adores the family dog, a close-up of the dog's face on her fridge will make her week.

Holiday and family gathering photos

Christmas lunch, Easter egg hunt, beach holidays — group photos work if you crop in close so faces are clear.

Gift Set Ideas

  • The Grandkid Set (3-6 magnets): One close-up of each grandchild. Simple, personal, perfect.
  • The Year in Review (6-12 magnets): One photo from each month or major event of the year.
  • The Memory Mix (6 magnets): 3 new photos + 3 favourite older photos — a mix of then and now.
  • The Pet Pack (3-6 magnets): All the family pets. More popular than you'd think.

Keeping a Memory Alive

We want to mention something that comes up more often than you might expect.

Some of our customers order magnets of a grandparent who has passed away. A favourite photo of Dad. A candid of Mum laughing. A wedding photo from decades ago.

These magnets become a quiet, daily reminder of someone they love. Not tucked away in an album — right there, in the heart of the home, every time they reach for the milk.

We think that's one of the most meaningful things a photo magnet can be.
