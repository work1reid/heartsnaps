How to Choose the Best Photo for Your Custom Magnet (And What to Avoid)

March 2026 6 min read Tips
📸

Your custom magnet is 63.5mm x 63.5mm — roughly the size of a large coaster. At that size, every detail matters. A photo that looks stunning on your phone screen might lose all its impact when printed small and stuck on a fridge.

We've printed thousands of magnets, and we've learned what works. Here's everything you need to know to get the best result.

The #1 Rule: Get Close

The single biggest factor in a great magnet photo is how close the subject fills the frame.

A portrait where someone's face takes up most of the image? That prints beautifully. A landscape shot of a beach with tiny people in the distance? The people become unrecognisable dots.

Think of it this way: if you can't clearly see the details when you hold your phone 30cm from your face, those details won't show on a magnet either.

Resolution: What You Need to Know

You don't need to understand DPI or megapixels. Just follow these guidelines:

Quick Resolution Guide

  • From your phone camera: Any photo taken directly with an iPhone or Android camera from the last 5 years is high enough resolution. You're fine.
  • From a photographer: Professional photos are always high-res. Use the original file they gave you, not a resized preview.
  • From social media: This is where problems happen. Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp all compress images heavily. A photo downloaded from Instagram is often only 30-40% of the original quality.
  • From a screenshot: Screenshots of photos are lower quality than the original. Always use the actual photo file.

The Instagram/Facebook Problem

This is the most common issue we see. Someone has a beautiful photo on their Instagram, they screenshot it or download it, and upload it for a magnet. The result is noticeably blurry because Instagram strips out most of the image data.

What to do instead:

  • Find the original photo in your phone's camera roll
  • If someone else took it, ask them to send you the original via AirDrop, Google Drive, or email — not via Instagram DM or WhatsApp
  • If a photographer took it, use the high-res files they delivered (usually via a gallery link or USB)

Lighting: What Prints Well

Great lighting for magnets

  • Natural daylight — photos taken outdoors or near a window almost always print well. The light is even and flattering.
  • Soft, even lighting — overcast days are actually perfect. No harsh shadows, no squinting.
  • Golden hour — that warm light just before sunset gives a gorgeous warm tone to prints.

Tricky lighting to watch for

  • Backlit photos — where the light source is behind the subject (e.g., standing in front of a window). Faces can appear very dark.
  • Harsh flash — direct flash can wash out skin tones and create unnatural shadows.
  • Very dark/nighttime photos — these can look fine on a bright phone screen but lose detail when printed. If the photo was taken in a dark restaurant or at night, check if faces are clearly visible.

Composition: What Works at 63.5mm

Remember, your magnet is square. Our upload tool lets you crop any photo to a square, but it helps to think about composition:

Photos that work brilliantly

  • Head-and-shoulders portraits — one or two people, faces filling most of the frame
  • Pet close-ups — dogs, cats, horses — a close-up of their face prints beautifully
  • Baby and toddler photos — close-up of a little face, first birthday smash cakes, tiny hands
  • Food and flat-lays — coffee art, a plate of food, a styled flat-lay
  • Macro/detail shots — flowers, rings, textures

Photos to think twice about

  • Large group photos — more than 4-5 people and faces become very small. Crop to a smaller group if possible.
  • Wide landscapes — they can work as art pieces, but recognisable subjects need to be large in the frame.
  • Photos with important text — small text becomes illegible at magnet size.

Colour vs Black & White

Both print beautifully. Black and white photos actually tend to look especially good as magnets — they have a timeless, artistic quality and the contrast translates well to print.

If you're unsure, colour is the safe choice. But if you have a great B&W portrait, don't hesitate — it'll look stunning.

Quick Checklist Before You Upload

  • Is it the original photo (not a screenshot or social media download)?
  • Can you see faces/details clearly when you zoom out on your phone?
  • Is the subject close enough to fill most of the frame?
  • Is the photo well-lit (not too dark, not washed out)?
  • If it's a group shot, are there 4 or fewer people?

If you answered yes to all of those, your magnet is going to look great.
