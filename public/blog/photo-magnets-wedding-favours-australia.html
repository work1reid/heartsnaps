Custom Photo Magnets as Wedding Favours: The Complete Australian Guide

March 2026 10 min read Wedding
💒

Here's the thing about most wedding favours: they end up in a drawer, in the bin, or forgotten on the reception table. Sugared almonds, personalised candles, tiny bottles of bubbles — they're nice gestures, but they don't stick around.

Photo magnets do. Literally.

A custom photo magnet goes straight on the fridge and stays there for years. Every time your guests open the fridge, they see your photo — and they remember your day. It's a favour that actually becomes part of someone's home.

Why Photo Magnets Work as Wedding Favours

We've made magnets for dozens of weddings across Australia, and the feedback is always the same: "Our guests loved them." Here's why they work so well:

  • They're useful. Everyone has a fridge. A photo magnet isn't clutter — it's functional decor.
  • They're personal. Your photo, your moment, your faces. It's not a generic gift with your names stamped on it.
  • They last. Unlike flowers or food favours, a quality photo magnet keeps its colour and stays on the fridge for years.
  • They're compact. Easy to set at each place setting, easy for guests to slip into a pocket or handbag.
  • They start conversations. Guests often show them to friends and family: "Look what we got at Sarah and Tom's wedding."

Choosing the Right Photo

Your magnets are 63.5mm x 63.5mm — about the size of a large Post-it note. That's a beautiful size for a close-up, but it means some photos work better than others.

What works best

  • Engagement shoot portraits — usually the best option. Close-up, well-lit, both of you looking great.
  • Ceremony or reception candids — if you're ordering after the wedding (for thank-you gifts).
  • Detail shots — rings, bouquets, table settings can work beautifully if you want a more artistic feel.
  • Black & white photos — these print particularly well and add a timeless, elegant touch.

What to avoid

  • Wide group shots — faces become very small at magnet size. If you want a group shot, crop in.
  • Screenshots from social media — Instagram and Facebook compress images heavily. Always use the original file from your photographer.
  • Very dark or backlit photos — they tend to lose detail when printed small.
If you're not sure whether your photo will print well, just order — we check every image before printing and will contact you if we think there's a better option.

How Many Should You Order?

The simple answer: order the same number as your guest count, plus 5-10 extras.

The extras are for:

  • Last-minute additions to the guest list
  • A few to keep for yourselves (you'll want them)
  • Any that might get left behind on the night

When to Order

We recommend ordering at least 3 weeks before your wedding date. Here's why:

  • Production time: Each magnet is handmade to order — printed, cut, and pressed by our family. For wedding orders, we typically need 5-7 business days.
  • Shipping: Allow 3-5 business days for delivery anywhere in Australia.

If you're ordering after the wedding (using a ceremony or reception photo as a thank-you gift), there's no rush — but the sooner you order, the sooner your guests get a lovely surprise in the mail.

Creative Ways to Present Wedding Magnets

At each place setting

The most popular approach. Place one magnet face-up on each napkin or beside each plate. Guests discover them as they sit down, and it immediately starts conversations.

On a display board

Set up a magnetic board near the exit or guest book table with all the magnets arranged on it. Add a sign: "Take a memory home." Guests pick one up on their way out.

In a bowl or basket

Simple and casual. Place them in a nice bowl near the door with a handwritten sign. Works well for relaxed, outdoor, or rustic-style weddings.

Attached to thank-you cards

If you're sending thank-you cards after the wedding, slip a magnet inside each envelope. Your guests get a lovely surprise — and a photo they'll put on their fridge straight away.

As part of a gift box

Pair the magnet with a small local treat — a chocolate, a honey jar, a tea bag — in a little box at each seat. It becomes a curated favour rather than a standalone item.

Same Photo or Different Photos?

Same photo is by far the most popular choice for wedding favours. It's simpler to produce, and it means every guest takes home the same beautiful moment.

Different photos can work if you want each table to have a different image, or if you're using the magnets as a creative seating chart (each guest's magnet has a table number printed on a personalised photo).

Either way, just let us know in the order notes and we'll make it happen.
